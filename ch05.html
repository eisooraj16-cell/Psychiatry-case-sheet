⚠ Pitfalls & Exam Traps
  • Confusing past history with current HOPI (current episode = Ch.03)
  • Skipping inter-episode functioning (highest-yield schizophrenia vs BPAD discriminator)
  • Forgetting to draw a life chart in any episodic illness
  • Using "relapse" and "recurrence" interchangeably (they are NOT synonymous)
  • Skipping ECT history (patients/families don't volunteer it)
  • Not asking about TEAS (treatment-emergent affective switches)
  • Forgetting pregnancy/lactation in women of reproductive age
  • Listing medical illnesses without commenting on psychiatric implications

A
Past Psychiatric History
Episode-by-episode chronological documentation
▾
💡 WHY THIS MATTERS
Past episodes are the trajectory the patient is now in. Documenting them chronologically reveals pattern, course modifiers, and treatment response. The inter-episode functioning question (“Did you go back to fully being yourself?”) is the highest-yield single discriminator between schizophrenia (no return to baseline) and BPAD (return to baseline).
Disorder-Specific Tailoring
Predominant Polarity (ISBD)
The polarity of ≥2/3 of all lifetime episodes. Predicts treatment response: depressive predominant → lamotrigine, quetiapine, lurasidone preferred; manic predominant → lithium, valproate preferred.
Life Chart Method Selection
NIMH-LCM: Mood severity above/below euthymia baseline. Best for bipolar and recurrent unipolar. Monthly granularity retrospective, daily prospective.
Tulsa LC: 8 developmental epochs with mood ratings, residences, relationships, substance exposure. Transdiagnostic.
Clozapine Eligibility Criteria
≥2 adequate trials of different antipsychotics at therapeutic dose for ≥6 weeks each, with poor response. “Adequate” = therapeutic dose + sufficient duration + adherence confirmed.
Document any depot/long-acting injectable antipsychotics tried, doses, durations, response
STAR*D Staging (Sequenced Treatment Alternatives to Relieve Depression)
Stage 1: Citalopram monotherapy
Stage 2: Switch to bupropion, sertraline, venlafaxine OR augment with bupropion/busPIRone
Stage 3: Mirtazapine or nortriptyline switch/augment
Stage 4: Tranylcypromine or venlafaxine+mirtazapine, or ECT
Adequate trial = therapeutic dose for ≥4-6 weeks with confirmed adherence.
Document each detox: substance, location, complications (DTs, seizures), year
Inpatient/outpatient rehab programs attended, duration, outcomes
Methadone, buprenorphine, naltrexone trials - doses, durations, response
Document: repeated A&E attendances, multiple short admissions, repeated DSH, crisis frequency pattern across years, relationship instability pattern
Number of sessions, modality (individual/group), who delivered, response
Document each SSRI/SNRI trial: drug, max dose reached, duration, response, reason for discontinuation
B
Past Medical & Surgical History
Organic mimics, treatment contraindications, drug interactions
▾
💡 THREE REASONS THIS MATTERS
  • Organic mimics: Hypothyroidism → depression; Hyperthyroidism → anxiety/mania; Cushing's → depression/psychosis; SLE → psychosis; Wilson's → mood + movement (young adults); NMDA-R encephalitis → first-episode psychosis with seizures
  • Treatment selection: Renal impairment → limits lithium; Hepatic impairment → limits valproate, carbamazepine, mirtazapine; Cardiac disease → limits TCAs, QT-prolonging antipsychotics; Pregnancy → contraindicates valproate
  • Drug interactions: SSRIs + NSAIDs/anticoagulants → bleeding; Lithium + NSAIDs/ACEi → toxicity; Carbamazepine + OCPs → contraceptive failure; Carbamazepine + warfarin → loss of anticoagulation
⚓ CLINICAL ANCHOR
In the elderly, the ratio of drug interactions to therapeutic gain rises sharply. The Beers criteria and the START/STOPP framework are the bedside tools for psychotropic prescribing in older adults. Mention them by name in viva when the case is geriatric.

How to Link Medical to Psychiatric
The examiner wants the link, not just the list. For each condition, state: (1) organic mimic risk, (2) treatment constraint, (3) interaction risk.
Example: “Type 2 DM → limits olanzapine (metabolic worsening), requires baseline HbA1c monitoring, increases vascular dementia risk.”

C
Life Chart Construction
NIMH-LCM, Tulsa LC, visual documentation
▾
💡 WHY LIFE CHARTS MATTER
Episodic illnesses are about pattern, not snapshot. A life chart turns scattered episodic data into a single visual sweep showing trajectory, treatment response, hospitalisation density, and life-event coupling. Drawing a life chart unprompted in viva is a high-impact discriminator demonstrating clinical maturity.
Pattern seen: depression-first? Delayed emergence of mania? Life-event coupling? Treatment response timing? Rapid cycling?
📈 NIMH-LCM Construction
Construction Instructions (7 Steps) ▸
  1. Set axes: Horizontal = time (months retro / days pro); Vertical = mood severity with euthymia baseline in middle
  2. Above baseline: Hypomania → Mania → Severe mania → Mania with psychosis (4 tiers)
  3. Below baseline: Depression in same 4 tiers (mild → moderate → severe → with psychotic features)
  4. Hospitalisations: Filled/darkened blocks for instant recognition
  5. Uncertain dates: Dotted outlines
  6. Treatments: Horizontal bars above mania zone (drug name + dose)
  7. Life events: Marked with impact rating -4 (very negative) to +4 (very positive)
Severity Coding Rule
NIMH-LCM codes severity by functional impairment, not symptom intensity alone. This is the rule that gives the chart its clinical power. An episode with fewer symptoms but complete work impairment codes higher than one with many symptoms but maintained functioning.
List each episode with: type (mania/hypomania/depression/mixed), start date, end date, severity tier, functional impact
Drug name, dose, start date, end date, plotted as horizontal bar above mania zone
Admission date, discharge date, voluntary/involuntary, MHCA section
Event, date, impact rating (-4 very negative to +4 very positive)

🎤 Viva Presentation Notes
💡 HOW TO PRESENT IN VIVA
Open with format and time span: “Sir, this is a retrospective NIMH-LCM-style life chart for this patient over [X] years, age [Y] to [Z]. Hypomanic states above baseline, depressive below. Hospitalisations as diamonds, treatments as horizontal bars, life events as marked points.”

Walk chronologically: onset → evolution → treatment response → switch points → hospitalisations → life-event coupling → current status.

End with diagnostic implication using ISBD vocabulary: “The chart shows a [pattern], consistent with [diagnosis] and a [predominant polarity] per ISBD nomenclature.”

Sample Phrasings
Interview questions and documentation language
▾
Episode-By-Episode Probe (Opening)
“When was the very first time you felt unwell with anything to do with your mind, mood, or behaviour? What was happening in your life at that time?”
Use this to anchor the chronological sequence, then work forward episode by episode.
Inter-Episode Functioning Probe (HIGHEST YIELD)
“Between the time you got better from that episode and the time you became unwell again, were you fully back to yourself? Were you working? Were your relationships back to how they were before? Or did some part of you feel changed?”
This single question does most of the diagnostic work: schizophrenia = incomplete recovery/progressive deterioration; BPAD = return to premorbid baseline.
Treatment Adherence Probe
“For how long did you take the medication? Did you ever stop and start again? What made you stop?”
Distinguish true treatment resistance from pseudo-resistance due to non-adherence.
ECT Probe (Patients Don't Volunteer This)
“Have you ever been given a treatment where you were put to sleep briefly and given a small electrical impulse, sometimes called shock treatment or ECT? When was that, how many sessions, and did it help?”
Frame non-stigmatisingly. Document: number of sessions, bilateral/unilateral, response, memory side effects.
Suicide Attempt / DSH Probe
“Did you ever do anything to harm yourself, or take anything you knew could hurt you? What did you do? What did you intend to happen? What happened next?”
Frame method, intent, outcome explicitly. Lethality = combination of method + intent. Document what happened next (A&E, admission, treatment change).
