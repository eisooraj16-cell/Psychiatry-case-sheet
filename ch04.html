Chapter 04

Negative History

The Adult Psychiatry History-Taking Compendium — NIMHANS Framework

🔤 Mnemonic: MMP-PNN-A-OTE-SCO

▼
Mood depressive Mood manic Perceptual
Philosophical (delusions) Negative symptoms
Network anxiety Attack OCD Onslaught trauma
Taste eating Engine substance Science cognition Chemistry organic

Twelve domains, one mnemonic, no domain forgotten. In viva order.

💬 Transition Phrase

▼

"I'd like to ask you now about a few other things that may not have come up so far, things I ask everyone, just to make sure we have a complete picture. Please tell me as briefly or as fully as you'd like."

This framing converts the sweep from a checklist into a conversation. Without it, disclosure rates fall.

⚠️ Cardinal Pitfalls (Top 5)

▼
  1. Saying "no other complaints" without screening — the cardinal sin. Itemise every domain.
  2. Confusing HOPI focused negatives with systematic negative history — two different sections, both required.
  3. Forgetting tobacco — most-missed substance; ask smoked and chewed separately.
  4. Skipping organic rule-out in first-episode psychosis — TSH, B12, UDS, metabolic panel.
  5. Not screening for prior unrecognised hypomania in depression — highest-stakes single miss; reframes case as bipolar II.

🎯 Disorder-Specific Rule-Outs at a Glance

▼

Schizophrenia / FEP

  • Mood disorder with psychotic features
  • Substance-induced (especially cannabis)
  • Organic / secondary psychosis
  • Schizoaffective (chronology check)

Mania / Bipolar I

  • Substance-induced mania (steroids, stimulants)
  • Organic mania (hyperthyroidism, frontal lesion)
  • Schizoaffective bipolar type

Major Depression

  • Prior unrecognised hypomania (bipolar II)
  • Substance-induced (alcohol #1 comorbidity)
  • Medical causes (TSH, B12, anaemia)
  • Uncomplicated bereavement

OCD

  • Psychotic delusions (insight is discriminator)
  • ASD restricted behaviour (ego-syntonic)
  • Normal cultural religious practice
  • OCRD spectrum (BDD, hoarding, etc.)

Anorexia Nervosa

  • Hyperthyroidism
  • Type 1 diabetes mellitus
  • Tuberculosis, IBD, HIV
  • Occult malignancy, Addison's

FND / Dissociative

  • Organic neurological disease (highest stakes)
  • Factitious disorder / malingering
  • Stone positive signs: Hoover, give-way, entrainment

A1
Mood, Depressive Pole
Persistent sadness, anhedonia, hopelessness, biological functions, SI
▼
📋 Why this matters

Depression is the most universal psychiatric screen. Subsyndromal dysthymia, bereavement-related depression, irritable depression in men, and somatic depression in older patients are all commonly missed.

✓ Negative ⚠ Positive Found ? Suspected — Not Screened
Document the sentinel response, follow-up findings, biological symptom review, and clinical reasoning. Note any subsyndromal dysthymia, bereavement-related depression, or irritable depression in men.
ℹ️ Sentinel Questions & Sample Phrasings
Sentinel & Follow-up
English

"Apart from what you have already told me, has there been a time recently when you have felt persistently sad or low, or lost interest in things you used to enjoy, for two weeks or more?"

Hindi

"Jo aapne abhi tak bataya hai uske alawa, kya kabhi aisa hua hai ki aap do hafton ya us se zyada dinon tak lagataar udaas mehsoos kiya ho, ya jin cheezon mein pehle aapko maza aata tha unmein ab dilchaspi nahin lagti ho?"

Hinglish

"Jo aapne bataya uske alawa, kya kabhi aisa hua hai ki aap continuously do hafton se zyada sad ya low feel kiya ho, ya jo cheezein pehle enjoy karte the, woh ab interesting nahin lagti?"

Follow-up if positive: "For how long? What about sleep, appetite, energy? Did you ever feel that life was not worth living, or have any thoughts of hurting yourself?"

High-yield missed items:

  • Subsyndromal dysthymia presented as "that's just how I am"
  • Bereavement-related depression dismissed as normal grief
  • Depression in a man presented as irritability rather than sadness
  • Depression in an older patient presented as somatic complaints
A2
Mood, Manic & Hypomanic Pole
Elevated mood, irritability, decreased sleep, grandiosity, pressured speech
▼
📋 Why this matters

CRITICAL: This is the prior-episode hypomania screen, not just current state. A single past unrecognised hypomania reframes a depressive case as bipolar II and changes management entirely. Irritable mania in women and adolescents is often missed.

✓ Negative ⚠ Positive Found ? Suspected — Not Screened
CRITICAL domain: Document sentinel response, informant verification, and any past episodes thoroughly. A single past unrecognised hypomania reframes the case as bipolar II. Note duration in days and family observations.
ℹ️ Sentinel Questions & Pitfalls
Sentinel & Follow-up
English

"Has there ever been a period in your life, even a short one, when you felt unusually high or unusually energetic, needed less sleep than usual but didn't feel tired, talked more than usual, or did things that were unusual for you?"

Hinglish

"Kya kabhi aisa time aaya hai life mein, chota period bhi, jab aap unusually high ya energetic feel kar rahe the, sleep kam thi but tired nahin lag rahe the, normal se zyada baatein kar rahe the, ya aisi cheezein kar rahe the jo aapke liye usual nahin thi?"

Follow-up: "How long did that last? Did the family say anything about it at the time? Did you spend money in unusual ways, make new plans, talk about big ideas, get into arguments?"

Highest-stakes single miss in the entire negative history: A single past unrecognised hypomania in a current depressive presentation reframes the case as bipolar II spectrum and changes management.

A3
Psychosis, Perceptual Disturbances
AH (3rd/2nd person, command, commentary, echo), VH, olfactory, gustatory, somatic
▼
📋 Why this matters

Hallucinations across all modalities must be screened. Hypnagogic/hypnopompic hallucinations require explicit elicitation. Religious/spiritual experiences and transient stress-induced perceptual disturbances in BPD are real but do not constitute primary psychosis.

✓ Negative ⚠ Positive Found ? Suspected — Not Screened
Document each modality screened, AH characteristics (inside/outside head, gender, content, command vs commentary), and clinical interpretation. Explicitly note hypnagogic/hypnopompic hallucination screening.
ℹ️ Sentinel Questions
English

"Have you ever heard voices speaking to you when there was no one around, or seen things that other people could not see?"

Hinglish

"Kya kabhi aapko aisi voices sunai di hain jab around koi nahin tha, ya aisi cheezein dikhi hain jo doosre log dekh nahin sakte the?"

Follow-up: "How often? In what voice, a man, a woman, someone you know? What did the voices say? Did they speak about you or to you? Were the voices inside your head or outside?"

A4
Psychosis, Delusions & Thought Alienation
Persecutory, referential, grandiose, religious, jealousy, somatic, nihilistic, control, erotomanic; FRS
▼
📋 Why this matters

Delusions of jealousy in alcohol use disorder (morbid jealousy) is very high-stakes for spousal safety. Folie-à-deux, delusional parasitosis in older patients, and first-rank symptoms in family members need careful teasing apart.

✓ Negative ⚠ Positive Found ? Suspected — Not Screened
Document each delusion type and FRS screened, conviction level, impact on behaviour, informant discrepancies, and any morbid jealousy or delusional parasitosis.
ℹ️ Sentinel Questions & High-Yield Misses
English

"Have you ever felt that someone was trying to harm you, or follow you, or that people were talking about you when they were not? Have you ever felt that your thoughts were being put into your head from outside, or taken out, or broadcast so that others could hear them?"

Hinglish

"Kya kabhi aisa feel hua hai ki koi aapko harm karna chahta hai, ya follow kar raha hai, ya log aapke baare mein baatein kar rahe hain jab actually aisa nahin tha? Kya kabhi aisa laga hai ki aapke thoughts bahar se aapke mind mein insert ho rahe hain, ya nikaale ja rahe hain, ya broadcast ho rahe hain?"

Follow-up: "Tell me more about that. When did it start? How sure are you about it now? Has anything happened that has made you doubt it, or are you still completely sure?"

High-yield missed: Morbid jealousy in alcohol use disorder; delusional parasitosis in older patients presenting to dermatology; folie-à-deux accepted as cultural; first-rank symptoms in family members confused with patient's own.

A5
Psychosis, Negative Symptoms
Avolition, alogia, affective blunting, asociality, anhedonia (negative-symptom sense)
▼
📋 Why this matters

Negative symptoms in a depressed-presenting first-episode psychosis case are often mistaken for depression itself. Prodromal flat affect described by family as "he just stopped talking" is a critical early indicator. Cannabis use in young adults can mask negative symptoms.

✓ Negative ⚠ Positive Found ? Suspected — Not Screened
Document each negative symptom screened, informant observations, differentiation from depressive picture, timeline in months, and primary vs secondary distinction.
ℹ️ Sentinel Questions
English

"Has there been a change in how active or expressive you are, has the family said you have become more withdrawn, less talkative, or less interested in things, in a way that doesn't feel like sadness?"

Hinglish

"Kya aapke being active aur expressive mein koi change aaya hai, family ne kaha hai ki aap zyada withdrawn ho gaye hain, kam talk karte hain, less interested ho gaye hain, lekin yeh sadness jaisa feel nahin hota?"

Follow-up: "How long has this been going on? Is it the same as when you have been sad before, or different? Are you still able to plan for tomorrow, look forward to things, get yourself out of the house?"

A6
Anxiety, Panic & Phobic Disorders
Free-floating anxiety, panic attacks, anticipatory anxiety, agoraphobia, social anxiety, specific phobias
▼
📋 Why this matters

Panic disorder presenting as cardiac complaints with normal workup is common. Agoraphobic avoidance documented as "she has stopped going out" without the anxiety component named misses the diagnosis. Benzodiazepine use bought OTC for chronic anxiety is often missed in the substance screen.

✓ Negative ⚠ Positive Found ? Suspected — Not Screened
Document each subtype screened, panic characteristics (duration, autonomic symptoms, fear of dying/going crazy), avoidance severity, and functional impact.
ℹ️ Sentinel Questions
English

"Apart from what you have already told me, do you have problems with anxiety, worry, or fear that comes on suddenly? Are there places or situations that you avoid because they make you anxious?"

Hinglish

"Jo aapne bataya uske alawa, kya aapko anxiety, worry, ya suddenly aane wala fear hota hai? Kya aisi places ya situations hain jinhe aap avoid karte hain because of anxiety?"

Follow-up: "Tell me about a recent episode. Did your heart race, did you feel short of breath, did you feel as if you were going to die or lose control? How long does it last? Are there warning signs?"

A7
OCD & Obsessive-Compulsive Related Disorders
Obsessions, compulsions, BDD, hair pulling, skin picking, hoarding
▼
📋 Why this matters

Religious scrupulosity dismissed as normal devotional practice; sexual obsessions in young men dismissed as sexual difficulty; childhood-onset OCD carried for years and never named; body dysmorphic disorder presenting to dermatology or cosmetology rather than psychiatry. Family accommodation is often not recognised as part of the illness.

✓ Negative ⚠ Positive Found ? Suspected — Not Screened
Document obsession types, compulsion types, time consumed per day, distress, resistance, family accommodation, functional impairment, and insight level.
ℹ️ Sentinel Questions
English

"Do you have thoughts that come into your mind against your will, that you find unpleasant or upsetting and that you cannot get rid of? Do you have to do certain things repeatedly, washing, checking, counting, arranging, even when you know it doesn't make sense?"

Hinglish

"Kya aapke mind mein against your will aise thoughts aate hain jo unpleasant lagte hain aur jinhe aap remove nahin kar paate? Kya aapko kuch cheezein repeatedly karni padti hain, washing, checking, counting, arranging, jab aap khud know karte hain ki yeh make sense nahin karta?"

Follow-up: "How much time per day do you spend on these? How distressed do you feel if you cannot do them? Does the family help you do them, or stop you from doing them? Have you tried to resist?"

A8
Trauma & Stressor-Related Disorders
Index trauma, intrusion, avoidance, NACM, hyperarousal, dissociative symptoms
▼
📋 Why this matters

Childhood sexual abuse is almost never volunteered. Domestic violence in women requires explicit framing. Intergenerational trauma (Partition, communal violence, displacement) is often not framed as the patient's own trauma. Medical trauma from ICU stays or difficult births is frequently missed.

✓ Negative ⚠ Positive Found ? Suspected — Not Screened
Document trauma type, Criterion A, symptom clusters with examples, timeline, functional impact, and any childhood/developmental trauma or intergenerational trauma.
ℹ️ Sentinel Questions
English

"Has anything very frightening or distressing ever happened to you, an accident, an assault, a serious illness, the death of someone close, or something that you don't think about often because it is too painful?"

Hinglish

"Kya aapke saath kabhi koi very frightening ya distressing event hua hai, accident, assault, serious illness, kisi close person ki death, ya kuch aisa jiske baare mein aap zyada think nahin karte because it is too painful?"

Follow-up: "After that, did you have nightmares, or sudden vivid memories that came up without warning? Did you start avoiding places or people that reminded you of it? Did you feel jumpy or on edge? How long has this been going on?"

A9
Eating & Feeding Disorders
Restriction, binge eating, compensatory purging/exercise, body image disturbance, weight/shape overvaluation
▼
📋 Why this matters

Atypical anorexia nervosa at normal BMI meets cognitive criteria without dramatic weight loss. Eating disorder in young Indian women dismissed as fashion-related dieting. Binge eating in obesity missed because the patient is not underweight. ARFID in adults presented as lifelong picky eating.

✓ Negative ⚠ Positive Found ? Suspected — Not Screened
Document eating patterns, binge/purge frequency, body image preoccupation, weight history (current, lowest, highest), medical complications, and menstrual status if applicable.
ℹ️ Sentinel Questions
English

"Apart from what you have already told me about your appetite, have you ever had a problem with your eating, restricting your food more than you needed to, eating very large amounts in one go and feeling out of control, or doing anything to compensate for what you ate?"

Hinglish

"Jo aapne apni appetite ke baare mein bataya uske alawa, kya aapko eating se related koi problem hui hai, zaroorat se zyada apni food restrict karna, ek baar mein bahut zyada khaana aur out of control feel karna, ya jo khaaya uske liye kuch compensate karna?"

Follow-up: "How long has this been going on? What is your current weight, and how do you feel about your body? Have you been weighing yourself, exercising to compensate, vomiting, using laxatives, or skipping meals?"

A10
Substance Use
Tobacco, alcohol, cannabis, opioids, sedatives/BZDs, stimulants, inhalants, cough syrup, regional substances
▼
📋 Why this matters

Tobacco is the most-missed substance because the candidate asks about "substances" and the patient does not consider tobacco one. Always ask separately for smoked and chewed forms. Benzodiazepines and OTC sleeping tablets bought without prescription are missed because patients consider them medicine. Cough syrup misuse in adolescents is rising. Cannabis in older patients is often dismissed as historical when it is current.

✓ Negative ⚠ Positive Found ? Suspected — Not Screened
Document every substance explicitly asked (tobacco smoked AND chewed separately), pattern, quantity, route, dependence criteria, withdrawal history, medical complications, family concerns, and timeline.
ℹ️ Sentinel Questions
English

"I'd like to ask about anything you might use, tobacco, alcohol, anything else. People use these for many different reasons, and I'm not here to judge. I want to understand the full picture. Do you smoke or chew tobacco in any form? Do you drink alcohol? Have you ever used cannabis, opium, sleeping tablets, or anything else?"

Hinglish

"Main aapse kuch cheezon ke baare mein poochna chahta hoon, tobacco, alcohol, ya kuch aur. Log inhe different reasons se use karte hain, aur main judge karne nahin aaya hoon. Main poora picture samajhna chahta hoon. Kya aap kisi form mein tobacco use karte hain? Kya aap alcohol lete hain? Kya kabhi cannabis, opium, sleeping tablets, ya kuch aur use kiya hai?"

Follow-up: "For how long? How much, how often? Have you ever tried to stop? What happened when you stopped, any shaking, sweating, sleeplessness? Has anyone in the family said anything about your use?"

A11
Cognitive & Neurocognitive
Memory, attention, executive function, IADL/ADL impairment, topographical disorientation, language
▼
📋 Why this matters

Subjective cognitive decline in a middle-aged depressed patient may be early MCI rather than depressive cognitive slowing. Long-term anticholinergic medication cognitive complaints are often dismissed as part of the illness. The "reverse" presentation where the family complains and the patient denies almost always indicates real organic decline.

✓ Negative ⚠ Positive Found ? Suspected — Not Screened
Document each cognitive domain with examples of impairment, informant observations, functional impact, onset, course, and any screening scores (MMSE/MoCA). Note patient vs informant discrepancy.
ℹ️ Sentinel Questions
English

"Have you noticed any change in your memory or your thinking, forgetting things you would normally remember, getting confused with things you used to do easily, getting lost in places you know well, or finding it hard to follow conversations?"

Hinglish

"Kya aapne apni memory ya thinking mein koi change notice kiya hai, aisi cheezein bhool jaana jo normally yaad rehti hain, jo kaam pehle easy the unmein confuse hona, familiar places mein lost ho jaana, ya conversation follow karne mein difficulty hona?"

Follow-up: "When did this start? Has it been getting worse, staying the same, or coming and going? Has the family noticed it as well? Is it interfering with daily activities, cooking, banking, finding your way?"

A12
Organic, Neurological & Metabolic
Head injury, seizures, focal signs, thyroid, B12, metabolic, current medications
▼
📋 Why this matters

Hypothyroidism presenting as treatment-resistant depression — TSH should be checked even if family history is negative. B12 deficiency in vegetarian patients with cognitive complaints. Steroid use (oral, inhaled, injected, topical) missed because the patient does not consider it psychiatric medication. OCP-related mood change in young women. Head injury from road traffic accident decades earlier dismissed as resolved.

✓ Negative ⚠ Positive Found ? Suspected — Not Screened
Document all neurological, medical, and medication screens. List every medication by name and dose. Note any steroid or hormonal agents. Record head injury details with LOC duration.
ℹ️ Sentinel Questions
English

"Have you ever had a serious head injury with loss of consciousness, a seizure, a stroke, or any other neurological problem? Do you have any current medical conditions like thyroid disease, diabetes, or hypertension, and what medicines are you currently taking, including anything from the chemist, herbal preparations, or anything from a traditional practitioner?"

Hinglish

"Kya kabhi aapko serious head injury hui hai jismein loss of consciousness ho, koi seizure, stroke, ya koi aur neurological problem? Aapko abhi koi medical condition hai jaise thyroid, diabetes, hypertension, aur is time kya medicines le rahe hain, chemist se kuch, herbal preparations, ya kisi traditional practitioner se mili hui cheezein bhi include karke?"

Follow-up: "When did that happen? How long was the loss of consciousness? What treatment did you receive? Are you on any treatment now? Has it caused any lasting problems?"

🎯 Section E: Disorder-Specific Tailoring

Focused rule-out lists for common index diagnoses. Use these to cross-check your negative history documentation.

E
Disorder-Specific Tailoring Reference
Quick rule-out lists by index diagnosis
▼

1. Schizophrenia / FEP

  • Mood disorder with psychotic features (chronology, mood-psychosis tie)
  • Substance-induced (esp. cannabis — highest misdiagnosis rate)
  • Organic psychosis (head injury, seizures, autoimmune, thyroid)
  • Schizoaffective (psychosis without mood ≥2 weeks)

2. Mania / Bipolar I

  • Substance-induced (steroids, stimulants, dopamine agonists)
  • Organic mania (hyperthyroidism, lupus, frontal lesions)
  • Schizoaffective bipolar type (psychosis outside mood episodes)

3. Bipolar Depression

  • Prior unrecognised hypomania (§D2 — the key discriminator)
  • Hypothyroidism, B12 deficiency
  • Substance-induced depression

4. Major Depressive Disorder

  • Prior unrecognised hypomania → bipolar II
  • Substance-induced (alcohol #1 comorbidity in India)
  • Medical causes (TSH, B12, anaemia, occult malignancy, post-stroke)
  • Uncomplicated bereavement

5. Alcohol Use Disorder

  • Primary mood/anxiety/trauma driving alcohol (self-medication)
  • Polysubstance (esp. benzodiazepines without prescription)
  • Medical complications (Wernicke's, hepatitis, cardiomyopathy, GI bleed)
  • Alcohol-induced psychiatric syndromes

6. Panic Disorder

  • Medical causes (hyperthyroidism, arrhythmia, asthma, pheochromocytoma)
  • Substance-induced (caffeine, stimulants, cannabis withdrawal, BZDs)
  • Primary mood/psychotic with secondary anxiety

7. OCD

  • Psychotic delusions (insight is discriminator)
  • ASD restricted behaviour (ego-syntonic vs distressed)
  • Normal cultural religious practice
  • OCRD spectrum (BDD, hoarding, hair pulling, skin picking)

8. PTSD

  • Adjustment disorder (shorter, less severe, no 4-cluster picture)
  • Primary depression (trauma incidental)
  • Dissociative disorder (dissociation primary, trauma developmental)
  • Malingering (compensation contexts)

9. Anorexia Nervosa

  • Hyperthyroidism, type 1 DM, TB, IBD, HIV
  • Occult malignancy, Addison's disease
  • Depressive weight loss (no body image disturbance)
  • Fasting glucose at intake

10. FND / Dissociative

  • Organic neurological disease (highest-stakes rule-out)
  • Factitious disorder / malingering (intentional production)
  • Stone positive signs: Hoover, give-way, entrainment

11. BPD-emphasised

  • Bipolar II (duration: days-weeks vs hours-days)
  • Complex PTSD (index trauma temporal anchoring)
  • ADHD (developmental history, cognitive profile)

12. Delirium vs Dementia

  • Delirium: depression with cognitive slowing, psychosis with preserved attention
  • Dementia: depressive pseudodementia, late-onset psychosis, delirium superimposed

⚠️ Section F: Pitfalls & Exam Traps

1. Saying "no other complaints" without screening

The cardinal sin. The candidate has not done the screen and is hoping the examiner will not ask. Every domain must be itemised.

2. Confusing HOPI focused negatives with systematic negative history

Two different sections with two different functions. HOPI negatives screen within the index domain. Chapter 04 screens between every major domain. If only one is present, the case is incomplete.

3. Forgetting tobacco in substance screening

The most-missed substance in psychiatric histories and the most relevant prognostically. Ask smoked and chewed separately.

4. Skipping organic rule-out in first-episode psychosis

TSH, B12, urine drug screen, and basic metabolic panel are the floor. The candidate who has not screened the medical differential cannot defend the primary psychiatric diagnosis.

5. Skipping medical rule-out in eating disorders

TB, hyperthyroidism, type 1 DM, occult malignancy, and IBD all present with weight loss and must be considered before AN is named as the cause.

6. Documenting negatives in vague terms

"No psychotic symptoms" is not adequate. Itemise: "no AH/VH/delusions/thought alienation/passivity/negative symptoms." The itemisation is what makes the documentation count as evidence.

7. Assuming the patient has volunteered all positives

Patients do not volunteer things they do not consider symptoms: past hypomania, childhood OCD, cannabis use, perimenopausal mood change. The screen catches the un-volunteered.

8. Treating the negative history as a checkbox

The sentinel-plus-follow-up structure is the difference between a screen that produces clinical data and a screen that produces a list of "no" answers.

9. Accepting a negative answer when the family answer differs

Psychotic patients deny symptoms; manic patients deny elevation; substance users minimise use; demented patients confabulate normality. The informant's parallel screen is essential.

10. Not screening for prior unrecognised hypomania in depression

The single highest-stakes miss in the entire negative history. A positive answer reframes the case as bipolar II and changes the management.
